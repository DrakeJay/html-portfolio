The CAD/CAM Room:

Welcome to our CAD/CAM Room, where innovation and precision converge. In the past, dental prosthetics were painstakingly crafted by hand using wax and traditional casting methods. Today, we've embraced cutting-edge CAD/CAM technology to digitally design and mill dental prosthetics with unmatched accuracy and efficiency.

This room represents a technological leap forward, allowing us to provide you with dental restorations that not only meet but exceed your expectations. Experience the future of dentistry in "The CAD/CAM Room," where tradition meets innovation, shaping your smile with care.

The Staining Room:

Staining is the crowning touch in the creation of a dental prosthesis, where our focus shifts to elevating the aesthetics of the tooth and gingiva, paying meticulous attention to factors like shade, translucency, and any unique characteristics requested by our clients.

Stains, available in either powder or pre-mixed form, are skillfully applied to enhance the natural appearance of the prosthesis. They then undergo a crucial phase in the oven to fully set, followed by a final bake with glaze to achieve a smooth and glossy finish, ensuring that each restoration is a true work of art.

However, when it comes to PMMA (Polymethyl Methacrylate), the acrylic-based material cannot withstand the traditional oven process. In such cases, we employ specialized acrylic-based stains for both the tooth and gingiva, which are meticulously applied and cured under UV light to achieve the desired esthetic outcome.

3D Printing:

At Rochester Dental Lab, we harness the incredible capabilities of 3D printing technology to craft dental models, nightguards, and mockups that exceed industry standards. Our state-of-the-art 3D printers transform digital designs into physical reality with unparalleled accuracy. Dental models, essential for diagnostic purposes, treatment planning, and creating custom restorations, are now crafted with precision and consistency that was once unimaginable. Nightguards, crucial for protecting teeth from grinding during sleep, are tailored to each patient's unique bite pattern, ensuring maximum comfort and efficacy. Moreover, our 3D printing capabilities extend to crafting lifelike mockups, allowing patients and dentists to visualize and refine the final treatment plan, enhancing communication and ensuring satisfaction with the end result.

My Work:

CAD/CAM framework from Chrome Cobalt, a strong and biocompatible alloy. Once the framework is fabricated, layers of porcelain are painstakingly applied to mimic the natural appearance of teeth, focusing on shade, translucency, and shape to achieve a lifelike aesthetic. After thorough quality control checks and adjustments, the bridge undergoes a final baking process to fuse the porcelain layers and achieve the desired strength and aesthetics. The result is a durable, functional, and visually pleasing full arch bridge

Creating a Zirconia porcelain-stacked full arch bridge is a precision-driven process that combines the strength of Zirconia with the beauty of porcelain. It starts with a meticulous impression or digital scan of the patient's oral structure. Next, a CAD/CAM system is used to design the Zirconia framework, known for its durability and biocompatibility. Layers of porcelain are then carefully applied to the framework, focusing on shade, translucency, and shape. The bridge undergoes a final baking process to fuse the porcelain layers, resulting in a strong, functional, and visually appealing full arch bridge that seamlessly restores the patient's smile, combining both strength and beauty.

Using CAD/CAM technology, a precise design is created for the crown. Lithium Disilicate, a highly durable and translucent material, is then milled or pressed into the desired shape. After the crown is formed, it undergoes a meticulous coloring and staining process to match the natural shade and appearance of the patient's surrounding teeth. A final firing process ensures the crown's strength and lifelike aesthetics.
